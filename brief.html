Laser Scanning Project
Wireframe Brief
Website redesign brief

Laser Scanning

Redesign the website to improve organic enquiries, convert referrals more effectively and position the business as a complete engineering solutions provider rather than only a scanning company.

Primary objective

Make the business easier to understand, easier to trust and easier to contact.

Main business context

Most work comes from repeat customers and recommendations, but the website still needs to improve organic enquiry generation.

Platform direction

Design in Figma. Build direction is Webflow, using the wireframe and references below to guide the final site.

Core problem

The current website does not communicate the full business clearly enough

Visitors do not immediately understand the breadth of capability, the wider engineering support offering or the best next step. The new site needs to validate trust quickly for referred traffic while also improving conversion from organic visitors.

Within 5 seconds, a visitor should understand that Laser Scanning can help capture, understand, validate and manufacture parts, and that it is easy to send a project over for review.
Positioning

What the business should feel like

Not just a scanning company

The website should move away from presenting the business as a narrow service provider.

A one-stop engineering solutions provider

The business should feel capable of supporting the wider engineering journey, from data capture through to manufacture and project support.

Lead with engineering problems and outcomes first. Support those with methods, tools and capability, not the other way around.
Capability structure

Group the business into clear top-level service buckets

The website needs to simplify a broad offering into a structure that users can understand quickly, while still allowing room for detailed pages underneath.

Data Capture

  • Handheld laser scanning
  • Portable laser scanning
  • CMM laser scanning
  • Touch probe CMM
  • CT scanning

Reverse Engineering

Inspection & Validation

Manufacturing & Prototyping

Engineering Support

  • DFM

  • Manufacturing advice
  • Supporting partners
  • End-to-end project assistance
Target users

Who the site needs to work for

Referral visitors

Already have some level of trust, but use the website to validate credibility and confirm capability.

Organic search visitors

Have a specific engineering problem and are looking for the right route forward, not just a particular piece of equipment.

Existing customers

Already trust the business and simply need a fast, low-friction way to send another enquiry or upload files.

Use the live wireframe as the primary structure

The live wireframe should guide the homepage layout and user flow. The Elementor site should be treated as secondary inspiration for spacing, section separation and general presentation style.

  • Build trust quickly

Key message

Sector positioning

Show range without pretending to be specialists in every market

Positioning approach

The website should position the business as adaptable, technically capable and focused on understanding the engineering challenge and the client’s required outcome.

Automotive Infrastructure Manufacturing Energy Industrial Equipment Specialist Projects

Important note

Avoid building weak generic sector pages that say very little. Use real case studies, use cases and examples to show sector range and capability instead.

Organic enquiry growth

Build search visibility around real engineering problems

Organic growth should come from pages that reflect real buyer intent and technically credible services, not generic awareness content.

Examples of strong page topics

  • Reverse engineering without CAD
  • High-accuracy GD&T inspection
  • CT scanning for internal geometry
  • Large structure scanning
  • Recreating legacy parts
  • Prototype to production support

Why this matters

These pages attract higher-intent traffic and support better-quality enquiries than vague educational posts or very broad service descriptions.

Design and UX direction

What the final site should feel like

Design direction

  • Clean
  • Structured
  • Technical but clear
  • Confident, not flashy
  • Built around real visuals
  • Consistent across pages

UX principles

  • Make the next step obvious
  • Reduce friction to contact
  • Show credibility early
  • Help visitors self-identify quickly
  • Balance breadth of service with clarity
Success criteria

How success should be judged

More website enquiries

Especially from organic traffic and referred visitors.

Lower bounce rate

Visitors should understand the offer faster and stay engaged for longer.

Stronger positioning

The business should feel like a capable engineering partner, not a narrow specialist supplier.
